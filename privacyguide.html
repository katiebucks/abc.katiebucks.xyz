Privacy Guide

Take control of your digital footprint before it controls you.

🔍 The Internet is Not Your Friend

Every search, message, and click is tracked—by your ISP, Big Tech, advertisers, and even governments. Privacy isn’t just for tech enthusiasts—it’s about protecting your data, identity, and freedom.

Think this is exaggerated? Watch Snowden. The surveillance exposed years ago was bad. Now, it’s worse.

🧠 The Power of Inferred Data

It’s not just about what you share—it’s about what companies can deduce.

  • You never posted about job hunting, but after searching for resume templates, LinkedIn ads flood your feed.
  • You never shared political views, but your browsing history signals a preference, and suddenly your social media nudges you toward certain narratives.
  • You never disclosed financial struggles, but your purchases and ad clicks flag you for predatory loan ads.

It’s not just about data you provide—it’s about the patterns they detect.

🕵️‍♂️ Who’s Watching & How They Use Your Data

Entity How They Use Your Data
📡 Governments Predict social behavior, track dissent, and monitor personal connections.
💰 Advertisers Push products before you even realize you “need” them.
🏢 Employers Analyze online behavior before hiring.
🏦 Banks & Insurers Adjust interest rates and policies based on digital footprints.

🔒 Essential Privacy Tools

These tools help reduce tracking and protect your digital life:

  • Tor Browser – Anonymizes browsing through multiple encryption layers.
  • Brave Browser – Blocks trackers and ads by default.
  • DuckDuckGo – Search without Google’s tracking.
  • Signal – End-to-end encrypted messaging, even governments can’t read.
  • ProtonMail – Encrypted email for true privacy.
  • Bitwarden – Secure password manager to protect your logins.

🏴‍☠️ Dark Web 101: How to Use Tor

  1. Download Tor Browser – Get it from torproject.org.
  2. Install & Open – Set it up like any other browser.
  3. Click "Connect" – Routes traffic through encrypted relays.
  4. Visit .onion Sites – Try katiebucks.onion for anonymous browsing.
  5. Don't Log into Personal Accounts – Using Google or Facebook on Tor defeats the purpose.
  6. Disable JavaScript – Prevents tracking and identity leaks (adjust this in settings).

💡 Pro Tip: Never download anything from the dark web unless you fully understand the risks. Malicious files can expose your real identity—or worse.

🤦‍♀️ Privacy Fails to Avoid

  • Reusing Passwords – A single data breach could expose all your accounts.
  • Not Encrypting Files – If your device is stolen, unprotected data is an open book.
  • Downloading Unverified Software – If it’s not from an official source, assume it’s malware.
